iPhone 18 Pro Max overheating affects performance. Cool down your device with these 12 solutions.

## Why iPhone 18 Pro Max Overheats

- A20 Pro chip demanding tasks
- AI processing and Neural Engine
- 5G Advanced and WiFi 7
- Pro display with high brightness
- Heavy gaming and AR
- iOS 19 early bugs
- MagSafe charging heat

## 12 Fixes for iPhone 18 Pro Max Overheating

### 1. Remove Case During Charging
Cases trap heat. Remove to let heat escape.

### 2. Enable Low Power Mode
Settings > Battery > Low Power Mode. Reduces chip heat.

### 3. Close Background Apps
Swipe up from bottom, close all apps. Background processes generate heat.

### 4. Reduce Screen Brightness
Lower brightness reduces chip workload.

### 5. Turn Off 5G Advanced
Settings > Cellular > Voice & Data > 5G Auto (not always on).

### 6. Avoid Direct Sunlight
Never leave iPhone in hot environments.

### 7. Update iOS
Thermal management improvements in updates.

### 8. Use Official Charger
Apple-certified accessories only.

### 9. Restart Weekly
Weekly restarts clear heat-causing processes.

### 10. Check Battery Health
Settings > Battery > Battery Health. Below 80% may cause heat.

### 11. Reset Network Settings
Settings > General > Reset > Reset Network Settings.

### 12. DFU Restore
Last resort for persistent software heating issues.

## When to Contact Apple Support

- Overheats during light use
- Phone won't cool down after 30 minutes off
- Battery drains rapidly while hot
- Visible swelling

## FAQ

**Q: Is iPhone 18 Pro Max supposed to get hot?**
A: Warm during heavy use is normal. Hot during normal use needs attention.

**Q: Does MagSafe cause overheating?**
A: MagSafe generates heat. Remove case during MagSafe charging if overheating.

## Conclusion
Most overheating resolves with Low Power Mode and restarts. Persistent issues need Apple support.
