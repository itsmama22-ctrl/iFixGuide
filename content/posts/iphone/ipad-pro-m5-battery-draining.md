iPad Pro M5 battery drain frustrates users. Maximize battery life with these 12 solutions.

## Why iPad Pro M5 Battery Drains Quickly

- M5 chip demanding tasks
- ProMotion 120Hz display
- 5G cellular always on
- Brightness too high
- Background app refresh
- iPadOS 18 bugs

## 12 Fixes for iPad Pro M5 Battery Drain

### 1. Enable Low Power Mode
Settings > Battery > Low Power Mode. Extends battery hours daily.

### 2. Reduce Screen Brightness
Control Center > Lower brightness or use Auto-Brightness.

### 3. Turn Off 5G When Unnecessary
Settings > Cellular > Cellular Data Options > Voice & Data > LTE.

### 4. Limit Background Refresh
Settings > General > Background App Refresh > Off for unused apps.

### 5. Set Auto-Lock Sooner
Settings > Display & Brightness > Auto-Lock > 2 minutes.

### 6. Disable Location Services
Settings > Privacy > Location Services > Off for unused apps.

### 7. Use Dark Mode
Settings > Display & Brightness > Dark. OLED displays save power.

### 8. Update iPadOS
Battery optimizations in updates. Settings > General > Software Update.

### 9. Check Battery Usage
Settings > Battery > Battery Usage. Identify problematic apps.

### 10. Close Unused Apps
Swipe up from bottom > Close unused apps.

### 11. Reduce Motion
Settings > Accessibility > Motion > Reduce Motion.

### 12. Reset Network Settings
Settings > General > Transfer or Reset > Reset > Reset Network Settings.

## Optimal iPad Pro M5 Battery Settings

- Auto-brightness: ON
- Low Power Mode: ON when below 40%
- Background refresh: Selective
- Auto-Lock: 2 minutes
- Dark Mode: ON

## FAQ

**Q: Is it normal for iPad Pro M5 to drain fast during use?**
A: Heavy apps drain fast. But idle drain over 5% hourly indicates issue.

**Q: Does iPadOS 18 drain battery more?**
A: Some early version bugs exist. Update to latest iPadOS 18 version.

## Conclusion
Battery issues usually resolve with Low Power Mode and settings adjustments. Persistent drain may need service.
