Bluetooth connectivity issues on iPhone 16 prevent wireless connections. Fix pairing problems with these solutions.

## Common iPhone 16 Bluetooth Issues

- Can't find Bluetooth device
- Pairing fails repeatedly
- Connected but no audio
- Connection drops randomly
- "Bluetooth incompatible" error
- AirPods won't connect

## 10 Fixes for iPhone 16 Bluetooth

### 1. Toggle Bluetooth
Settings > Bluetooth > Off, wait 10 seconds, On.

### 2. Restart Both Devices
Restart iPhone and the Bluetooth device.

### 3. Forget and Re-Pair
Settings > Bluetooth > tap (i) > Forget This Device. Re-pair from scratch.

### 4. Reset Network Settings
Settings > General > Transfer or Reset > Reset > Reset Network Settings.

### 5. Update iOS
Bluetooth stack updates in iOS versions. Keep updated.

### 6. Check Device Compatibility
Ensure Bluetooth device supports current Bluetooth standards.

### 7. Reset Bluetooth Module
Airplane Mode on/off resets Bluetooth hardware.

### 8. Check for Interference
Microwave, WiFi routers, cordless phones can interfere. Move away from sources.

### 9. Reset All Settings
For persistent Bluetooth issues, Settings > General > Reset > Reset All Settings.

### 10. Update Device Firmware
Some devices (AirPods, Beats) need firmware updates via Apple Settings.

## AirPods Specific Fixes

- Reset AirPods: Hold setup button 15 seconds
- Forget device, close AirPods case, wait 30 seconds, re-open
- Check Battery > General > About > AirPods firmware version

## FAQ

**Q: Why won't my AirPods connect to iPhone 16?**
A: Reset AirPods, forget device, ensure case is charged. Close and reopen case proximity.

**Q: Does iOS 18 have more Bluetooth bugs?**
A: Some users report Bluetooth issues after updates. Updates usually fix these.

## Conclusion
Most Bluetooth issues resolve with forget/re-pair or restart. Persistent hardware issues need service.
