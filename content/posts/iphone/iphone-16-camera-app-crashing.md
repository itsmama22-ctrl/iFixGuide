Camera app crashes prevent you from capturing moments. Fix iPhone 16 camera issues with these solutions.

## Why iPhone 16 Camera Crashes

- Lens calibration issues
- iOS 18.x bugs
- Corrupt camera app cache
- Storage full
- Hardware malfunction
- Third-party camera apps conflicting

## 11 Fixes for iPhone 16 Camera Crashing

### 1. Force Close Camera App
Swipe up from home, find Camera, swipe up again to force close. Reopen.

### 2. Restart iPhone
Standard restart clears app glitches.

### 3. Check Storage Space
Settings > General > iPhone Storage. Need at least 1GB free for camera to work properly.

### 4. Update iOS
Apple cameras improvements in updates. Settings > General > Software Update.

### 5. Reset Camera Settings
Settings > General > Transfer or Reset > Reset > Reset All Settings.

### 6. Clear Safari Cache
Settings > Safari > Clear History and Website Data. Cached data can affect camera.

### 7. Test in Safe Mode
Restart holding volume down to see if third-party app conflicts exist.

### 8. Delete Photos
Delete old photos if storage critically low. Or use iCloud Photos to free space.

### 9. Reset Network Settings
Network issues can affect camera processing. Settings > General > Reset > Reset Network Settings.

### 10. Update All Apps
Outdated apps can conflict. Update via App Store > Account > Update All.

### 11. DFU Restore
Complete restore resolves persistent software camera issues.

## Alternative Camera Solutions

- Use built-in Photos app instead of camera shortcut
- Try Snapchat or Instagram camera if main app fails
- Use Control Center camera shortcut
- Voice Control to open camera (Settings > Accessibility > Voice Control)

## FAQ

**Q: Why does camera crash only in certain modes?**
A: Specific lens or mode issue. Try Photo mode first, then test each mode separately.

**Q: Does iOS 18 have more camera bugs?**
A: Some users report camera crashes after iOS 18 updates. Updates usually fix these.

## Conclusion
Camera crashes usually resolve with restart or storage cleanup. Persistent issues may require DFU restore or Apple support.
