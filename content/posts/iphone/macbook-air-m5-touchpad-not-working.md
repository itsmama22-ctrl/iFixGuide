Touchpad issues on MacBook Air M5 disrupt workflow. Fix trackpad problems with these solutions.

## Common MacBook Air M5 Touchpad Issues

- Touchpad not responding
- Click not working
- Gestures not detected
- Cursor jumping
- Touchpad stuck
- Multi-touch not working

## 8 Fixes for MacBook Air M5 Touchpad

### 1. Clean Touchpad Surface
Clean with microfiber cloth. Dirt and oils affect sensitivity.

### 2. Check Touchpad Settings
System Settings > Touchpad. Ensure all settings enabled.

### 3. Reset NVRAM/PRAM
Shut down > Power on holding Option + Command + P + R for 20 seconds.

### 4. Reset SMC
Intel Mac: Shut down > Power on holding Option + Control + Shift + Power 10 seconds.
Apple Silicon: Shut down > Wait 30 seconds > Power on.

### 5. Boot in Safe Mode
Shut down > Power on holding Shift. Safe mode tests for software conflicts.

### 6. Check for macOS Updates
Touchpad drivers in updates. System Settings > Software Update.

### 7. Reset All Settings
System Settings > General > Transfer or Reset > Reset > Reset All Settings.

### 8. Contact Apple Support
Hardware failure requires service or replacement.

## Touchpad Gestures MacBook Air M5

- Click: Tap or press
- Right-click: Two-finger click or corner click
- Scroll: Two-finger swipe
- Zoom: Pinch
- Expose: Four-finger swipe down
- Switch apps: Four-finger swipe left/right

## FAQ

**Q: Why did touchpad stop working suddenly?**
A: Software glitch usually. SMC reset often fixes.

**Q: Can I use an external mouse instead?**
A: Yes, USB or Bluetooth mouse works as workaround.

## Conclusion
Most touchpad issues resolve with SMC reset or settings adjustment. Hardware issues need service.
